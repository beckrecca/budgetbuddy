Budget Buddy

Welcome! Most budget calculators ask you to enter your monthly expenses and your monthly income, and then they do a simple subtraction. However, for those who have a tough time budgeting, it is helpful to take into account what you have now, when you get paid, and when bills are due. Budget Buddy helps you visualize the short term, with a table showing how your balance will change with the comings and goings of your finances. It also lets you add additional transactions, so you can imagine potential purchases.

First, use the column on the left to add your regular monthly bills. Then, use the column on the right to enter information about your income and available funds. Finally, select the date to which you would like Budget Buddy to forecast.

50/20/30 Rule

Many financial experts recommend the 50/30/20 Rule for budgeting one's money. The rule splits expenses into three categories: fixed expenses (like rent/mortgage, utilities, loan payments, subscriptions), financial goals (like saving for retirement or a down payment), and flexible spending (like groceries, dining out, shopping).
